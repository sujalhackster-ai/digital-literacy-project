🎮 Game Title: The Digital Fortress: Freshman Year
The Concept
A 2D point-and-click narrative adventure where you play as a first-year student navigating their first week at university. Instead of fighting monsters, your "HP" is your Digital Reputation and your "Armor" is your Cyber Hygiene.

Core Gameplay Mechanics
The Phishing Inbox (Mini-Game):

Action: You receive 5 emails in your university inbox. You must drag "Safe" emails to your study folder and "Report" suspicious ones to the IT desk.

The Twist: One email looks like a "Free Canteen Coupon" but has a slightly misspelled URL (vltbhopal.ac.in instead of vitbhopal.ac.in).

Learning: Teaches players to spot URL spoofing and phishing patterns.

The Permission Gate:

Action: To enter a campus party, you must first "audit" your Instagram profile. You are shown a mock profile and must click and "hide" 3 pieces of sensitive data (e.g., a photo of your room key, your phone number in the bio, or your location-tagged library desk).

Learning: Emphasizes the importance of private profiles and data minimization.

The 2FA Puzzle:

Action: A fast-paced "Connect the Dots" game where you must sync a randomly generated 6-digit code from your phone screen to your laptop login before a 15-second timer runs out.

Learning: Reinforces the habit of using Two-Factor Authentication for critical accounts.

How it Teaches Safety
Instant Feedback: If you click a phishing link, your screen "glitches" and shows a pop-up explaining exactly what red flag you missed.

Reputation Score: Your final score isn't based on gold, but on your "Trust Level." High scores unlock a "Digital Ambassador" badge at the end of the game.
